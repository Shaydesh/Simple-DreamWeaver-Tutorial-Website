Adding Additional Web Pages

The next logical step after creating your first web page is to create related pages that hold related content, or a website. Creating a template to generate new webpages from, is an efficient means of creating additional pages for your website. According to Jeffery Brown, Chris Minnick, and Susan Lane (2010), in the book Design Methodology and Technology (Adobe CS5 Web Edition), templates are a tool that provide a way to maintain a constistant look and layout for all of your pages (p. 21-9). This tutorial is going to walk you through the steps of creating a template and generating additional pages for your website with your newly created template.

So, you have now created a web page that captures the style that you want the rest of your website to project. The next step is to create a template out of your newley formed web page and generate additional pages from that template. The first thing you will do is click on the files tab and select save as template. After your template is created, you have to select the areas in your new template that will be editable. This is done by selecting inside the element in which you want to create an editable region, clicking on the insert tab, select template objects, and then editable region. Dreamweaver will prompt you to name your new editable region. After you have identified all the editable parts of your template, click on file and select save, you can now create new webpages based on your template. This is done by clicking on the file tab and selecting new. In the new document menus select "page from template", and select the template you created. A new page has now been created based on your template. Notice, only the editable regions that you identified in your template can be altered in this new page...Brown, J., Minnick, C., Lane, S. (2010). Design Methodology and Technology(Adobe CS5 Web Edition). Prosoft Learning.
